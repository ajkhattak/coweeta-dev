Description:
Abstract:
The data included consist of daily climatic information recorded at five climate stations at Coweeta Hydrologic Laboratory in Macon County, North Carolina, USA: CS01, CS17, CS21, CS28, and CS77. These stations are operated by the Southern Research Station, USDA Forest Service. Data include daily air temperature, relative humidity, barometric pressure (CS01 only), wind speed and direction, photosynthetically active photon flux density (PPFD), and solar radiation measurements (CS01 only) from 1983 through 2017 (starting year varies per climate station).
Purpose:
The Coweeta Hydrologic Laboratory was established in 1934 and is world-renowned for its research in forest hydrology. Coweeta was established to determine the fundamental effects of forest management on soil and water resources and to serve as a testing ground for theories in forest hydrology. To facilitate this, a network of climate and precipitation stations was established across the site. The research program has since expanded its focus to encompass watershed ecosystem science. The original climate and precipitation network continues to facilitate these studies and serves as the foundation of the long-term data record.

Methodology:
Methodology_Type: Field
Methodology_Description:
Digital daily air temperature data have been recorded at the main climate station (CS01) continuously since 1983. In addition to CS01, there are four climate stations across the basin (CS17, CS21, CS28, and CS77). Each of these stations continually measures and records (CR10X, Campbell Scientific, Inc., Logan, UT, USA) the following variables every 5 minutes: temperature and relative humidity in an open field (HMP45c, Campbell Scientific, Logan, UT, USA), photosynthetically active photon flux density (LI-190-SB, Campbell Scientific), as well as wind speed and wind direction at canopy height (014A and 024A, MetOne Instruments, Grants Pass, OR, USA). Barometric air pressure (Vaisala CS106, Campbell Scientific) as well as solar radiation (model 8–48, Eppley Lab, Inc., Newport, RI, USA). Data retrieval from the climate network is via wireless remote access. All data recorded to the CR10X datalogger are transmitted via radio frequency (Freewave Technologies, Inc., FGR-115RC, Boulder, CO) from each of the climate stations to a computer server in the data processing office.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Hourly averaging is done by the datalogger, and downloaded weekly. Daily averages are calculated on a monthly basis (from the 24 hourly averages) when the datasets are assembled and stored on the local server. Similarly, the minimum and maximum represent the absolute minimum or maximum 5 minute measurement for each day.

Wind speed and direction are obtained a little differently. In vector averaging, either the orthogonal components of the wind are measured directly with a wind instrument or the speed and direction are measured with an anemometer and a wind vane and then they are used to derive the orthogonal components. To obtain the vector-averaged speed and direction, the components are summed and vector averaged at the end of the averaging time.

Overview_Description:
Entity_and_Attribute_Overview:
Below you will find the data available in this data publication and a short description of its contents.

\Data\csXX_daily.csv (where XX = climate station number 01, 17, 21, 28, or 77)

These data contains daily climate data collected at the specified climate station (csXX) at Coweeta Hydrologic Laboratory. Data were recorded every 5 minutes and what is included in this file is the average (or minimum/maximum as noted) of those 5 minute measurements for each day.

YEAR = Calendar year of recorded measurement.

MONTH = Month of recorded measurement.

DAY = Day of recorded measurement.

TMIN = Daily minimum air temperature in degrees Celsius.
TMAX = Daily maximum air temperature in degrees Celsius.
TAVG = Daily average air temperature in degrees Celsius.

RHMIN = Daily minimum relative humidity as a % (0-100)*.
RHMAX = Daily maximum relative humidity as a % (0-100)*.
RHAVG = Daily average relative humidity as a % (0-100)*.

VPMIN = Minimum daily saturation air vapor pressure (datalogger calculates this based on air temperature and relative humidity according to Lowe [1977]) in millibars.
VPMAX = Maximum daily saturation air vapor pressure (datalogger calculates this based on air temperature and relative humidity according to Lowe [1977]) in millibars.
VPAVG = Average daily saturation air vapor pressure (datalogger calculates this based on air temperature and relative humidity according to Lowe [1977]) in millibars.

WIND = Daily average wind speed in meters per second (m/s).
W_DIR = Mean wind direction (azimuth, 0-360 degrees).

PPFD = Photosynthetically Active Photon Flux Density in micromoles of photons per meter squared per second.

TOTALSOLAR = Total Solar Radiation in langleys (CS17, CS21, CS28, and CS77 didn't start measuring this until 2016).

AVG_B_PRESS = Hourly air Barometric Pressure in kilopascals (kPa) (only available for CS01).

*NOTE: There are a few relative humitidy (RH) values that were recorded as > 100. We concluded it was best to leave the data as recorded, but those data points should really be 100.
